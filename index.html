About Me

Hi, I’m Faith — a multidisciplinary creative and emerging technologist based in the Midwest. My work lives at the intersection of research, storytelling, design, wellness, and technology. With a background spanning public health, higher education, community programming, and the arts, I’m interested in how people create meaning, build connection, and navigate increasingly complex social and digital worlds. I earned my B.A. in Women’s, Gender, and Sexuality Studies from The Ohio State University, where I developed an interdisciplinary foundation rooted in critical thinking, research, communication, and social analysis. I later completed a Master of Public Health, which expanded my experience across clinical, educational, and international settings.

Most recently, I’ve been focused on technology and software development. Through programs like G{Code} and Code the Dream, I discovered a strong interest in coding, particularly the blend of logic, systems thinking, creativity, and design. Since building my first webpage, I’ve become increasingly interested in the ways technology can expand creative expression and shape more thoughtful user experiences. Currently, I’m developing my skills in software engineering, backend development, and data analytics while exploring UX/UI and interactive storytelling. Long term, I’m interested in building digital experiences that merge narrative, aesthetics, and systems thinking — including a future narrative-driven video game inspired by emotionally rich and visually immersive titles like Neva and Celeste.
