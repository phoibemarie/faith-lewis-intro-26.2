Faith M. Lewis

About

Hi! My name is Faith. I am 27 years old, and I reside in the MidWest. I have a dog named Rusty, and we live in a cute apartment that I've deemed as "The Tree House". I have a dynamic background educationally and professionally. I've studied at Ohio State University as an alumn of the Younf Scholars Program. Here, I majored in Women's, Gender, and Sexuality Studies which engages diverse disciplines, such as literature, philosophy, history, sociology, psychology, and biology to analyze and solve problems from a variety of perspectives. My ambition is to be an innovative thinker and engaged citizen who understands the increasingly multi-faceted nature of the world through critical thinking, communication, collaboration, research, and leadership skills that can be applied across displines. Merged with a Master's of Public Health, I have worked in higher education, international relations, and clinical settings. Beyond that, I've been able to explore the world, having traveled to Kampala, Uganda and Chendgu, China which has ignited my passion for cultural exchanges and the social detemrminants of identity and wellbeing. Recently, I was a featured speakers at the Black Mental Health Summit, and I was awarded Best Poster Presentation by CHIPTS for my literature review of integrating Black Feminist Strategies into Mental Health Care in L.A., California.

My most recent goal has been to immerse myself in activities that encourage self-realization for myself and others. I have engaged in multiple artistic apprenticeships and grant opportunities provided by ArtsWave Cincinnati, ArtWorks Cincinnati, and URGE Ohio. I have also developed creative arts programs that promoted mental health and social wellness for teens and adults in clinical and non-clinical settings, such as ART Therapy, a group for people livin with HIV that I initiated a local community health clinic for 3 year. On a personal note, I've been able to display my work in exhbitions such as Hearts of Liberation Hallery and the Parts or Pieces Performance Art Gallery. My poetic works have been performed at local open mics and city-wide events with the Cincy Juneteenth Festival, the Underground Railroad Freedom Center, and most recently, Waiting to Exhale host by Kennedy Heights Art Center.

Now, I'm learning how to code. I realized my affinity for the skill through G{Code}, a program designed to provide BIPOC women and non-binary folks with the opportunity to develop technical skills in web development and data anlystics. Once I started to build my first webpage, I was gratified by the blend of logic, technology, and design. Since then, I have a mind toward the positive social impacts that technology could have on our societies, and the ways it can be used to expand our creative potential. In the short term, my goal is to develop my backend skills, and seek employment in data and development. As a personal project, I'd like to build a narrative videogame that blends the fun of puzzle-platforming and stunning visuals with satisfying storytelling, as seen in games like "Neva" and "Celeste".
